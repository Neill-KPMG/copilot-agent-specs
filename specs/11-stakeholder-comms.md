# Stakeholder Communications

## Purpose
Audience-tuned writing for leadership, teams, and cross-functional partners.

## Core Activities
- Draft executive updates and board-ready summaries
- Write team announcements and all-hands messages
- Produce status reports and project updates
- Compose risk escalations and incident communications
- Pick the right channel (email vs Teams) and tone for the audience

## Required Fields

| Field | Your Input | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Message type** | _(fill in)_ | Exec update, announcement, escalation, status, cross-functional update |
| **Audience** | _(fill in)_ | Specific group — leadership team, all-hands, customer success |
| **Key points** | _(fill in)_ | Bullet list of facts and messages to convey |

## Optional Fields

| Field | Your Input | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tone** | _(fill in)_ | Direct, reassuring, urgent, celebratory, etc. |
| **Channel** | _(fill in)_ | Email, Teams post, document, or recommendation |
| **Length** | _(fill in)_ | One-paragraph, three-bullet, full memo |
| **Call to action** | _(fill in)_ | What should the reader do after reading |
| **Supporting data** | _(fill in)_ | Specific numbers, quotes, links to include |

## Example

> Draft a risk escalation email to the VP of Engineering about the database migration delay — 3 bullets covering impact, mitigation, and ask — direct tone.

## Expected Output
Drafted message ready to review and send, or sent on your behalf.
